What this is

A team leader's view of their formation team, twelve weeks into the journey. Sample data shows an immigration team led by Sarah Martinez in Cincinnati, with five direct members spanning all five canonical profiles. Two of her team members have themselves invited others, sparking two sub-teams. One sub-team member has begun inviting too — the multiplication is starting to compound.

The architecture point. A "team" is not a separate object in the database. It's a participant plus everyone whose invited_by points to them. Every person who invites becomes a leader of their own team automatically. The invite graph IS the movement, and it grows organically. Sarah's reach (12) is larger than her team (5) because her team members are leading teams of their own.

The funder story. The platform is not a discernment tool that happens to track teams. It is a movement-building tool whose first instance forms people around immigration. The seven-dimension profiling, the discernment-driven team formation, the longitudinal data, and the gamified multiplication graph would work identically for racial reconciliation, creation care, or any movement requiring the church to move from passive awareness to formed communal action. Immigration is the proof of concept; the infrastructure is the asset.

What's not built yet. This dashboard is a static mockup. Once approved, real implementation depends on the v3.1 return loop (so participants can log in and see their team), the team-context fields (team name editing), and the recommendation engine that powers "what your team is ready to do together." All buildable; the data model already supports it.
